Before you integrate, it helps to understand how Spaire structures the world. Every object relates to the others in a predictable way, and knowing the shape of the data model makes the API, webhooks, and dashboard much easier to reason about.

## The data model

```
Organization
├── Products
│   ├── Prices  (fixed | free | custom | seat_based | metered_unit)
│   └── Benefits (license_keys | meter_credit | discord | downloadables | github_repository | custom)
├── Checkouts ──────────────────────────────────┐
├── Customers                                   │
│   ├── Orders  (created from checkouts) ←──────┤
│   └── Subscriptions (created from checkouts) ←┘
│       └── Benefit Grants (active entitlements)
├── Discounts
├── Meters (for usage-based billing)
└── Account (your payout account)
```

---

## Organization

Your organization is the root object in Spaire. Everything lives under it — products, customers, webhooks, access tokens, and your payout account.

Each organization has a **slug** (e.g., `acme`) that appears in your dashboard URL. You can run multiple organizations from a single Spaire account if you have separate products or brands, each with its own billing, customers, and payout account.

Key settings on an organization:
- **Default currency** — used as a fallback when currency can't be inferred from the customer's location
- **Proration behavior** — how mid-cycle subscription changes are charged (`invoice` immediately or add to the next billing cycle)
- **Subscription settings** — whether customers can update their own subscriptions, cancel behavior, and portal options

## Products and Prices

A **product** is what you're selling. It has a name, optional description, optional media, and one or more prices attached to it.

A **price** defines the billing terms. Spaire supports five price types:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `fixed` | A set amount charged on a recurring or one-time basis |
| `free` | No charge — useful for free tiers or trials |
| `custom` | Pay-what-you-want with optional min/max bounds |
| `seat_based` | Tiered per-seat pricing, billed per seat count |
| `metered_unit` | Billed per unit consumed, measured by a meter |

A product can have multiple prices — for example, a monthly price and a yearly price for the same plan. Spaire supports 36 currencies including USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD, JPY, BRL, INR, and more.

<Warning>
  Billing cycle and price type cannot be changed after a price is created. Create a new price if you need different terms.
</Warning>

## Checkouts

A **checkout** is a payment session — the moment a customer is about to pay. Spaire gives you three ways to create one:

| Method | How it works | Best for |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| [Checkout link](/features/checkout/links) | A permanent URL you share anywhere | Landing pages, emails, docs |
| [Embedded checkout](/features/checkout/embed) | A widget rendered inside your own page | Keeping customers in your UI |
| [Checkout session](/features/checkout/session) | Created server-side via the API | Custom flows, pre-filled fields, metadata |

All three produce the same outcome: a completed order or subscription linked to a customer in your account. Checkout sessions expire after one hour if not completed.

## Customers

A **customer** is a person or organization that has purchased from you or been created via the API. Spaire creates customers automatically on first checkout.

The most important field for integration is `external_id` — set it to your internal user ID when creating or updating a customer. Once set, it's immutable and unique per organization. This lets you look up billing state using your own identifiers instead of storing Spaire IDs:

```bash
GET /v1/customers/external/{your_internal_user_id}
```

Customers have a self-service **portal** where they can view orders, manage subscriptions, update payment methods, and access files and license keys — hosted by Spaire, no code required on your side.

---

## Orders

An **order** is the record of a completed transaction. Every payment — whether a one-time purchase or a recurring subscription renewal — produces an order.

Orders have a **billing reason** that tells you why they were created:

| Billing reason | When it happens |
|----------------|-----------------|
| `purchase` | One-time product bought |
| `subscription_create` | First payment when subscribing |
| `subscription_cycle` | Automatic renewal on each billing period |
| `subscription_update` | Plan change mid-cycle (proration) |
| `client_invoice` | Manual invoice created by the merchant |

Order statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `pending` | Payment initiated, not yet confirmed |
| `paid` | Payment successful |
| `refunded` | Fully refunded |
| `partially_refunded` | Partially refunded |

## Subscriptions

A **subscription** is the ongoing billing relationship for a recurring product. Each billing period, Spaire automatically creates an order and attempts payment.

Subscription lifecycle:

```
incomplete ──(payment confirmed)──→ trialing* ──(trial ends)──→ active
     └──(payment not confirmed)──→ incomplete_expired

active ──(payment fails)──→ past_due ──(retry succeeds)──→ active
                                  └──(all retries fail)──→ unpaid

active ──(canceled)──→ canceled   (access ends at period end)
active ──(revoked)───→ canceled   (access ends immediately)
```

`*` Only if the product has a trial period configured.

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `incomplete` | First payment initiated, awaiting confirmation |
| `incomplete_expired` | First payment was not confirmed in time |
| `trialing` | In free trial period, no charge yet |
| `active` | Billing normally |
| `past_due` | Payment failed, retrying automatically |
| `canceled` | Canceled — access continues until period ends |
| `unpaid` | All payment retries exhausted |

When a subscription ends, all benefit grants are automatically revoked.

## Benefits and Benefit Grants

A **benefit** is an automated entitlement you attach to a product. When a customer purchases that product, Spaire immediately creates a **benefit grant** — activating the entitlement without any code from you.

The six built-in benefit types:

| Type | What Spaire does automatically |
|------|-------------------------------|
| `license_keys` | Generates a unique key and delivers it to the customer |
| `meter_credit` | Adds credits to the customer's usage meter balance |
| `discord` | Assigns a Discord role via OAuth |
| `downloadables` | Grants access to files you've uploaded |
| `github_repository` | Grants access to a GitHub repository via OAuth |
| `custom` | Calls a URL you define to provision and revoke |

When a subscription is canceled or a refund is issued, Spaire automatically revokes all associated grants. For built-in types, no cleanup code is needed.

---

## Discounts

A **discount** reduces the amount charged at checkout. Discounts can be:

- **Percentage** — e.g., 20% off (or 100% for free access)
- **Fixed amount** — e.g., $10 off

They can apply **once**, **forever**, or for a **repeating** number of months. Discounts can be code-less (applied programmatically via the API) or given a redemption code that customers enter at checkout.

## Webhooks

Webhooks are how Spaire tells your application that something changed. Every significant state change emits a signed event you subscribe to on your server:

```
order.paid                → fulfill a purchase
subscription.active       → unlock access for a new subscriber
subscription.canceled     → schedule access removal
benefit_grant.created     → provision a custom entitlement
benefit_grant.revoked     → deprovision a custom entitlement
customer.state_changed    → a customer's access profile changed
```

All events follow the [Standard Webhooks](https://www.standardwebhooks.com) specification and are signed with a secret you verify on arrival. See the full [event reference](/integrate/webhooks/events).

---

## Account and Payouts

Your **account** is your payout account — linked to your Spaire organization. You connect it once during onboarding.

Your balance grows as orders settle after Spaire's fee. You can view your current balance under **Finance → Balance** and withdraw to your bank at any time from **Finance → Payouts**.

<Info>
  As Merchant of Record, Spaire is the legal seller on every transaction. We collect payment, remit taxes to the relevant authorities, and then pay out your earnings. See [how it works](/merchant-of-record/introduction).
</Info>
