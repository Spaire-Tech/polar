Invoices let you bill customers directly without requiring them to go through a checkout flow. Create a draft, review it, then finalize and share it — with an optional payment link so customers can pay online.

<Info>
  Invoices are designed for **sales-assisted billing** — situations where you've already agreed on a deal and need to send a formal invoice. For self-serve purchases, use [Checkout Links](/features/checkout/links) instead.
</Info>

## Invoice lifecycle

Invoices move through the following statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Editable. Not yet sent to the customer. |
| **Open** | Finalized and sent. Awaiting payment. |
| **Paid** | Payment confirmed. |
| **Void** | Cancelled before or after being sent. |

## Create an invoice

Navigate to **Sales → Invoices** and click **New Invoice**.

### Customer

Select an existing customer from your customer list, or create a new one inline. Once a customer is selected, the invoice currency selector appears — choose the currency you want to invoice in.

<Tip>
  You can invoice in any of the 30+ supported currencies, including USD, EUR, GBP, AED, JPY, and more.
</Tip>

### Billing address

Once a customer is selected, a billing address section appears. It auto-fills from the customer's stored billing address if one exists. The billing address is used for tax calculation and is printed on the PDF invoice.

### Line items

Add one or more line items. Each line item has:

- **Description** — what you're charging for
- **Quantity** — number of units
- **Unit amount** — price per unit in the invoice currency

You can populate a line item from an existing product in your catalog (which auto-fills the description and amount), or enter custom values. Use **Add item** to add more lines, and **New product** to create a product inline without leaving the invoice form.

### Discount

Optionally apply a discount from your catalog. Supports both percentage and fixed-amount discounts. You can also create a new discount inline.

The discount is applied to the invoice subtotal and shown as a line on the PDF.

### Payment link

Attach a checkout link to the invoice so the customer can pay online directly from it. When a payment link is included, the finalized invoice will contain a clickable **Pay now** button.

<Warning>
  Make sure the checkout link's currency matches the invoice currency. If they differ, a warning is shown — the customer would be charged in the checkout link's currency, which may be confusing.
</Warning>

You can select an existing checkout link or create one inline. See [Checkout Links](/features/checkout/links) for how to set them up.

### Details

Configure payment terms and additional references:

- **Payment due** — set a due date using the Net 7 / 15 / 30 / 60 shortcuts or pick a custom date
- **Memo** — a note printed on the invoice, visible to the customer
- **PO Number** — the customer's purchase order reference number
- **On behalf of** — override the seller name printed on the invoice (defaults to your organization name)

## Finalizing an invoice

Once you're happy with the draft, click **Finalize**. This:

1. Locks the invoice and assigns it an invoice number
2. Calculates tax automatically based on the billing address
3. Generates a PDF
4. Changes the status from **Draft** → **Open**

Finalized invoices cannot be edited. If you need to make changes, void the invoice and create a new one.

## Sending an invoice

After finalizing, use **Send** to deliver the invoice to the customer via Stripe. The customer receives an email with the invoice PDF attached and, if a payment link was attached, a button to pay online.

## Voiding an invoice

Both draft and open invoices can be voided. A voided invoice is permanently cancelled and cannot be re-opened.
